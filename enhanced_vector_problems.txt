###Problem Title: Understanding Vector Addition in R2
Problem Description: Given vectors u = [[3,5]] and v = [[2,-1]], explore vector addition

#Step 1 [Multiple Choice - Single]: What does vector addition represent geometrically?
Step Type: multiple-choice-single
Question: What does adding two vectors represent geometrically?
Options:
  A) Placing vectors head-to-tail and drawing from first tail to last head
  B) Multiplying the magnitudes of the vectors
  C) Finding the angle between the vectors
  D) Subtracting one vector from another
Correct: A
Explanation: Vector addition follows the parallelogram law or head-to-tail method, where the resultant vector goes from the tail of the first to the head of the last.

#Step 2 [Expression & Answer]: Write component form u + v
Step Type: step-by-step
Step Expression: [[3,5]] + [[2,-1]]
Step Expected: [[3,5]] + [[2,-1]]

#Step 3 [True/False]: Verify addition is commutative
Step Type: true-false
Question: Is u + v = v + u for any vectors u and v?
Answer: True
Explanation: Vector addition is commutative. Adding vectors in any order gives the same result.

#Step 4 [Expression & Answer]: Add corresponding components
Step Type: step-by-step
Step Expression: [[3,5]] + [[2,-1]]
Step Expected: [[5,4]]

  B) How much u and v point in the same direction (projection relationship)

###Problem Title: Cross Product - Geometric Understanding

#Step 1 [Multiple Choice - Multiple]: Select properties of cross product

###Problem Title: Applications - Force Resultant
Problem Description: Two forces F_1 = [[3,4]] N and F_2 = [[-1,5]] N act on an object. Find resultant and magnitude.

#Step 1 [Multiple Choice - Single]: What is a resultant force?
Step Type: multiple-choice-single
Question: What does the resultant force represent?
Options:
  A) The difference between the two forces
  B) The single force that has the same effect as all forces combined
  C) The average of all forces
  D) The largest force acting
Correct: B
Explanation: The resultant is the vector sum of all forces, representing their combined effect.

#Step 2 [Expression & Answer]: Calculate resultant F_1 + F_2
Step Type: step-by-step
Step Expression: [[3,4]] + [[-1,5]]
Step Expected: [[2,9]]

#Step 3 [True/False]: Direction matters in forces
Step Type: true-false
Question: In physics, the direction of a force is just as important as its magnitude. True or False?
Answer: True
Explanation: Forces are vectors - both magnitude and direction determine their effect.

#Step 4 [Expression & Answer]: Calculate magnitude of resultant
Step Type: step-by-step
Step Expression: sqrt(2^2 + 9^2)
Step Expected: sqrt(85)

#Step 5 [Multiple Choice - Single]: Interpret the result
Step Type: multiple-choice-single
Question: The resultant force magnitude sqrt(85) approx 9.22 N. How does this compare to the individual forces?
Options:
  A) It is less than both forces (vector cancellation)
  B) It is between the two force magnitudes
  C) It is greater than both individual forces (vector addition)
  D) It equals the sum of the magnitudes
Correct: C
Explanation: ||F_1|| = 5 N and ||F_2|| approx 5.1 N, but the resultant is approx 9.22 N because the forces point in similar directions (both have large positive y-components).

PEDAGOGICAL BENEFITS OF MIXED STEP TYPES:

1. Conceptual Understanding (Multiple Choice/True-False)
   - Students must understand WHY, not just HOW
   - Tests recognition of key concepts
   - Provides immediate feedback on misconceptions

2. Formula Mastery (Fill in Blank)
   - Reinforces important formulas
   - Tests recall without multiple choice hints
   - Helps memorization through active retrieval

3. Computational Skills (Expression & Answer)
   - Develops calculation proficiency
   - Shows step-by-step problem solving
   - Builds algebraic manipulation skills

4. Metacognition (Verification steps with True/False)
   - Students check their own understanding
   - Promotes self-monitoring
   - Connects calculations to concepts

5. Engagement (Variety)
   - Different question types maintain interest
   - Reduces monotony
   - Appeals to different learning styles
