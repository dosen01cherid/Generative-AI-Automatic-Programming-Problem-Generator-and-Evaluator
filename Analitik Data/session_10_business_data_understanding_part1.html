Slide 3

Business Understanding Framework

6 Key Components

1. Business Objectives

What does the organization want to achieve?

  • Strategic goals
  • Business metrics to improve
  • Timeline and constraints

2. Stakeholder Identification

Who are the key players?

  • Decision makers
  • Data owners
  • End users of analytics
  • Subject matter experts

3. Problem Definition

What is the specific problem to solve?

  • Current state vs. desired state
  • Root cause analysis
  • Problem scope and boundaries

4. Success Criteria

How will we measure success?

  • Quantifiable metrics
  • Target values
  • Baseline measurements

5. Constraints & Resources

What limitations do we have?

  • Budget constraints
  • Time constraints
  • Data availability
  • Technical infrastructure

6. Analytics Translation

Convert business problem → analytics problem

  • Identify analytics approach (descriptive, predictive, prescriptive)
  • Define target variable
  • Determine required data
Slide 4

SMART Objectives Framework

Every business objective should be SMART

SMART Criteria

Letter Meaning Description Example Question
S Specific Clearly defined, not vague What exactly do we want to achieve?
M Measurable Can be quantified How will we measure progress?
A Achievable Realistic given constraints Can we realistically achieve this?
R Relevant Aligned with business strategy Does this support our business goals?
T Time-bound Has a clear deadline When should this be accomplished?

Examples: Bad vs Good Objectives

❌ Bad (Not SMART)

  • "Increase sales"
  • "Improve customer satisfaction"
  • "Reduce costs"
  • "Be more profitable"

Problem: Vague, not measurable, no timeline

✓ Good (SMART)

  • "Increase monthly sales revenue by 15% within 6 months"
  • "Improve NPS score from 45 to 60 in Q2 2025"
  • "Reduce customer acquisition cost by 20% by end of year"
  • "Increase profit margin from 12% to 18% in 12 months"

Why: Specific, measurable, time-bound

Case Study: E-Commerce Customer Churn

Bad objective: "We want to reduce customer churn"

SMART objective: "Reduce monthly customer churn rate from 8% to 5% within 9 months by implementing targeted retention campaigns based on predictive churn model"

  • S: Reduce customer churn through retention campaigns
  • M: From 8% to 5% (measurable reduction)
  • A: 3% reduction is achievable with targeted campaigns
  • R: Aligned with business goal of customer retention
  • T: Within 9 months

Slide 6

Problem Definition

"A problem well-stated is half-solved" - Charles Kettering

The 5 Whys Technique

Teknik untuk menemukan root cause dari business problem

Example: Customer Churn

  1. Why are customers leaving? → They're not satisfied
  2. Why are they not satisfied? → Poor customer service
  3. Why is customer service poor? → Long wait times
  4. Why are wait times long? → Insufficient support staff
  5. Why is staff insufficient? → Budget constraints + high turnover

Root cause: Need to optimize staffing allocation and improve retention

Problem Statement Framework

Problem Statement Template:

"[Current State] is causing [Business Impact]. We need to [Desired State] by [Method/Approach] to achieve [Business Outcome] within [Timeframe]."

Problem Statement Components:

Component Description Example
Current State What's happening now "25% monthly churn rate"
Business Impact Why it matters "$2M annual revenue loss"
Desired State Target outcome "Reduce to 15% churn"
Method Analytics approach "Predictive churn model + retention campaigns"
Business Outcome Expected benefit "Save $800K annually"
Timeframe When "Within 12 months"

Complete Problem Statement Example:

Scenario: Online Retail Company

Problem Statement:

"Our current monthly customer churn rate of 25% is causing an estimated $2M in lost annual revenue and hindering our growth targets. We need to reduce the churn rate to 15% by implementing a predictive analytics model to identify at-risk customers and deploy targeted retention campaigns, which will save approximately $800K in annual revenue within 12 months."

Common Pitfalls in Problem Definition

❌ Avoid These Mistakes:

  • Too vague: "Improve customer experience"
  • Solution-focused: "Build a machine learning model" (this is HOW, not WHAT)
  • Not measurable: "Make customers happier"
  • No business context: "Predict customer behavior" (Why? What impact?)
  • Scope creep: "Solve all customer issues" (too broad)
Slide 7

Translating Business to Analytics Problems

The Translation Challenge

Business problems are expressed in business language. We need to translate them into analytics problems.

Business Language

  • Increase revenue
  • Reduce churn
  • Improve efficiency
  • Better targeting
  • Optimize inventory

Analytics Language

  • Regression (predict sales)
  • Classification (churn yes/no)
  • Optimization
  • Clustering (customer segments)
  • Time series forecasting

Analytics Problem Types

Analytics Type Question Answered Business Use Case Techniques
Descriptive What happened? Sales dashboard, KPI reporting Aggregation, Visualization
Diagnostic Why did it happen? Why did sales drop last quarter? Drill-down, Correlation analysis
Predictive What will happen? Churn prediction, demand forecasting Regression, Classification, Time series
Prescriptive What should we do? Price optimization, resource allocation Optimization, Simulation

Translation Framework

Step 1: Identify Business Goal

Example: "Reduce customer churn"

Step 2: Define Analytics Objective

Example: "Predict which customers will churn in next 30 days"

Step 3: Specify Analytics Type

Example: "Binary classification problem (Churn: Yes/No)"

Step 4: Define Target Variable

Example: "Target = 1 if customer churns within 30 days, 0 otherwise"

Step 5: Identify Required Features

Example: "Customer demographics, usage patterns, payment history, support tickets, etc."

Step 6: Define Success Metric

Example: "F1-Score > 0.75, Recall > 0.80 (capture 80% of churners)"

Complete Translation Example

Aspect Business View Analytics View
Goal Reduce customer churn from 25% to 15% Build predictive model with 80% recall
Question Which customers are likely to leave? P(Churn = 1 | Customer Features) > threshold
Action Target retention campaigns Score customers, rank by churn probability
Success Churn rate reduced by 10 percentage points Model recall ≥ 80%, precision ≥ 65%
Slide 8

Success Criteria & Metrics

Two Types of Success Metrics

1. Business Metrics

Measure business impact

  • Revenue increase
  • Cost reduction
  • Customer satisfaction (NPS, CSAT)
  • Market share
  • Operational efficiency

Example: "Reduce churn from 25% to 15%"

2. Model Performance Metrics

Measure analytics quality

  • Accuracy, Precision, Recall
  • F1-Score, AUC-ROC
  • MAE, RMSE, R²
  • Model interpretability
  • Inference time

Example: "F1-Score ≥ 0.75"

Important: Business metrics are ultimately more important than model metrics!

A model with 95% accuracy is useless if it doesn't improve business outcomes.

Baseline Measurement

Always establish a baseline before starting analytics project:

Improvement Calculation:

$$\text{Improvement} = \frac{\text{New Value} - \text{Baseline}}{\text{Baseline}} \times 100\%$$

Example:

Baseline churn rate: 25%

New churn rate: 15%

$$\text{Improvement} = \frac{15 - 25}{25} \times 100\% = -40\%$$

(40% reduction in churn rate)

Defining Good Success Criteria

Criteria Description Example
Quantifiable Can be measured with numbers "Increase sales by 15%" not "Increase sales"
Baseline Know starting point "From 25% to 15%" not just "15%"
Target Clear goal value "Achieve 15% churn" not "Reduce churn"
Timeline When to achieve "Within 12 months"
Verifiable Can be independently verified Use existing BI system to track churn

Business Impact Estimation

Churn Reduction ROI Calculation:

Given:

  • Current monthly churn: 25% of 10,000 customers = 2,500 customers
  • Average customer lifetime value (LTV): $800
  • Target churn: 15% = 1,500 customers

Calculation:

$$\text{Customers Saved} = 2,500 - 1,500 = 1,000 \text{ customers/month}$$

$$\text{Annual Revenue Saved} = 1,000 \times 12 \times 800 = \$9,600,000$$

$$\text{Project Cost} = \$500,000$$

$$\text{ROI} = \frac{9,600,000 - 500,000}{500,000} \times 100\% = 1,820\%$$

Expected ROI

1,820%

Strong business case for analytics project
