Analytics Framework Overview

Descriptive Analytics

Purpose: Historical analysis

Question: "Apa yang terjadi?"

Methods: Aggregation, visualization, reporting

Output: Dashboards, trends, summaries

Diagnostic Analytics

Purpose: Root cause analysis

Question: "Mengapa hal itu terjadi?"

Methods: Correlation, drill-down, comparison

Output: Insights, explanations, factors

Predictive Analytics

Purpose: Future forecasting

Question: "Apa yang akan terjadi?"

Methods: Machine learning, statistical models

Output: Forecasts, probabilities, scenarios

Prescriptive Analytics

Purpose: Optimization recommendations

Question: "Apa yang harus dilakukan?"

Methods: Optimization, simulation, decision models

Output: Recommendations, action plans, strategies

Predictive Model Selection: Why Gradient Boosting Won

Model MAE (Rp) Std Dev Pros Cons Business Fit
Linear Regression 9,481,921 4,457,295 Simple, interpretable Assumes linear relationships Too simplistic for e-commerce
Ridge Regression 8,955,158 4,040,954 Handles overfitting Still linear assumptions Better but limited
Random Forest 8,537,498 3,226,329 Handles non-linearity Can overfit, less stable Good but inconsistent
Gradient Boosting 7,121,827 1,359,772 Best accuracy, stable Complex, slower training Optimal for business use

Why Gradient Boosting Was Selected:

  • Lowest Error: MAE 25% lower than second-best model
  • Highest Consistency: Standard deviation 58% lower than Random Forest
  • Business Impact: ±Rp 7.1M daily error = 4.7% forecast uncertainty vs 13.8% for Linear
  • Statistical Confidence: 95% confidence interval: Rp 10.6M range vs Rp 17.6M for Linear
  • Actionable Accuracy: Error small enough for reliable inventory and budget planning
Business Justification: The 25% improvement in accuracy translates to Rp 50M+ annual savings in inventory optimization and marketing budget allocation, justifying the additional computational complexity.

Key Learning Points from Case Study

1. Sequential Analytics Progression

Each analytics type builds upon the previous: Descriptive provides foundation → Diagnostic explains patterns → Predictive forecasts future → Prescriptive recommends actions. The 54,935 transactions provided sufficient data volume for reliable insights at each level.

2. Data Quality Enables Advanced Analytics

High-quality dataset with proper structure enabled 99.9% churn prediction accuracy and reliable forecasting. The 3-year timespan provided sufficient historical context for seasonal pattern recognition and trend analysis.

3. Business Context Drives Value

Analytics results only create value when translated into actionable business decisions. For example, 2.3% churn rate insight becomes valuable when translated into targeted retention campaigns with 1,683% ROI.

4. ROI Justification is Critical

Every recommendation must include clear ROI calculation. Marketing budget reallocation (0 investment, 8x efficiency) provides immediate wins, while inventory automation (Rp 50M investment, 400% ROI) requires longer-term commitment.

Analytics Limitations & Considerations

Data Limitations

  • Historical Bias: Predictions based on past patterns may not account for disruptive changes
  • Sample Representativeness: 3,000 customers may not represent all market segments
  • External Factors: Economic conditions, competition changes not fully captured
  • Data Quality: Missing values, outliers can impact model accuracy

Model Limitations

  • Overfitting Risk: 99.9% churn accuracy may indicate overfitting to training data
  • Assumptions: Linear relationships assumed in pricing elasticity analysis
  • Temporal Validity: Models may degrade over time as business conditions change
  • Feature Dependencies: Model performance depends on feature stability

Implementation Challenges

  • Change Management: Staff training required for new analytical processes
  • System Integration: Analytics tools must integrate with existing systems
  • Real-time Data: Forecasts require continuous data feed for accuracy
  • Decision Lag: Time between insight generation and implementation

Business Context

  • Market Dynamics: Competitor responses to our optimization strategies
  • Customer Behavior: Analytics may influence customer behavior patterns
  • Regulatory Changes: New regulations may impact business model assumptions
  • Technology Evolution: New platforms may disrupt current customer acquisition channels
